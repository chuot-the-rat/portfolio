02 — Challenge

Design Constraints

Beverage packaging operates within strict constraints. The design needed to work on an actual shrink sleeve dieline while incorporating all required elements: nutritional information, barcode, bilingual text (English/French), volume markings, and recycling symbols.

Key Constraints

  • Work within shrink sleeve can dieline specifications
  • Maintain brand cohesion across three distinct flavours
  • Ensure readability at shelf distance (2-3 meters)
  • Balance creative expression with packaging compliance
  • Create differentiation without losing visual unity

The unconventional flavour names (Ponzu Zing, Kewpie Pop, Ginger Zinger) also presented a challenge: how to communicate these unexpected taste profiles visually while maintaining the playful brand personality.

03 — Visual System

Building the Design Language

The visual system uses strong colour blocking, simple illustrations, and flowing linework to suggest movement, fizz, and flavour. Each can follows the same structural template while using distinct palettes and ingredient-inspired graphics.

Design Principles

Colour Blocking: Each flavour uses a dominant colour that floods the can, creating immediate visual impact and shelf presence.

Ingredient Illustrations: Simple, graphic representations of key ingredients (ponzu citrus, kewpie bottle, ginger root) provide flavour cues without overcrowding.

Flowing Lines: Curved, organic linework suggests carbonation, movement, and the refreshing nature of the beverage.

04 — Solution

The Three Flavours

Ponzu Zing

Citrus-forward with umami depth. Orange and yellow palette with illustrated citrus slices and flowing lines that evoke tangy brightness.

Kewpie Pop

Creamy-sweet with a subtle savory twist. Pink and coral palette featuring the iconic kewpie bottle silhouette and playful bubble motifs.

Ginger Zinger

Spicy-refreshing with ginger kick. Green and lime palette with illustrated ginger root and energetic, zigzagging line work.

All designs were developed with production constraints in mind, ensuring that the creative vision could be realistically manufactured and distributed. The use of spot colors, proper trapping, and print-safe gradients ensure the designs would reproduce accurately in production.

07 — Reflection

Key Takeaways

This project reinforced the importance of designing within constraints. Rather than limiting creativity, the technical requirements of packaging design pushed me to find innovative solutions that work in the real world.

The most valuable lesson was learning to create clear, scalable systems that balance personality, consistency, and production constraints. Each design decision—from colour choices to illustration style—had to serve both aesthetic and functional purposes.

Working with dielines, compliance requirements, and print specifications gave me practical experience in production-ready design, bridging the gap between concept and manufactured product.
